Oneway Carpool

Oneway carpool offers a dependable and secure commuting solution for ride-sharing, while also helping you cut down on travel expenses and minimise your environmental impact by reducing your carbon footprint.

Tools:

Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Visual Studio Code

Timeline:

July - September 2022 (8 weeks)

Team:

Solo school project

Role:

UI/UX Designer, Graphic Designer

OVERVIEW

With the ever-increasing petrol prices in New Zealand and the heavy traffic on the roads, commuters are looking for ways to cut down on transportation costs and contribute to environmental sustainability. Carpooling emerges as an ideal solution to these challenges. Oneway carpool provides a user-friendly platform that enables individuals to quickly and conveniently locate and connect with like-minded individuals who are also interested in carpooling, whether for regular daily commutes or one-off trips.

PROBLEM

Many commuters face the dual challenges of high petrol prices and long wait times for public transportation, making it difficult to find an affordable and efficient way to get to their destinations. As a result, there is a growing need for a transportation solution that addresses both of these issues, allowing commuters to reduce their transportation costs while also minimizing their wait times. By providing a more accessible and convenient way to travel, this solution could greatly improve the overall commuting experience for many individuals.

"How might we provide a transportation solution that not only helps commuters save money in the face of rising petrol prices but also reduces wait times associated with traditional public transportation, ultimately enabling a more convenient and efficient commuting experience?"

SOLUTION

With the Oneway Carpool app, users can efficiently locate and connect with compatible carpool partners along their daily route, thereby reducing their transportation costs and commute times while also minimising their carbon footprint. Through the use of T2 and T3 transit lanes, Oneway Carpool optimises travel time and reduces traffic congestion, contributing to a more efficient and eco-friendly commuting experience. Overall, Oneway Carpool offers a simple and accessible solution for individuals looking to save money, time, and contribute to a more sustainable transportation system.

RESEARCH

To begin, I conducted online research to examine the current state of carpooling in New Zealand, as well as other ride-sharing platforms currently available. The following are key findings:

  • Every day, 1.4 million Kiwis drive to work, with the majority traveling alone. This leaves 5.5 million empty seats out of a total of 7 million seats available, indicating a significant potential carpooling market.
  • Only 37% of individuals aged 15 and above in New Zealand have utilized public transportation within the past year, and wait times can be lengthy.
  • While well-known ride-sharing applications such as Uber, Zoomy, and DiDi are available in New Zealand, they are costly. Additionally, there are some free ride-sharing platforms, but they can have confusing user interfaces.

With these findings in mind, I aim to develop an app that takes advantage of carpooling while remaining affordable for users. The goal of this app is to provide a platform for both drivers and riders to save money rather than earn money.

PERSONA

The insights I gained from the research leading up to the persona. The goal of creating a persona was to develop a deeper understanding of the target user's needs and goals, enabling me to design a more personalised and effective user experience.

By identifying patterns and pain points, the persona helped guide my decision-making around product features, design, and messaging to better meet the needs and expectations of the target audience.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Uber

Uber is a global ride-sharing service that offers a range of services to cater to a diverse user base. Its strengths include an extensive network, reliable service, and a user-friendly app.

Pain Point:

  • Overview your financial situation and understand how much you can spend daily, weekly or monthly
  • Set value goals to remind yourself when you want to make impulse buying
  • Monitor your goal progress and provide suggestions if you are not on track

Coseats.co.nz

Coseats.co.nz is a carpooling platform based in New Zealand that connects drivers with passengers for long-distance trips. It is an eco-friendly and affordable alternative to traditional ride-sharing services.

Pain Point:

  • Limited availability of drivers, which can make it difficult to find rides
  • Potential scheduling conflicts, as riders need to coordinate with drivers for long-distance trips
  • Safety concerns related to sharing rides with strangers, as users do not have control over who they ride with

Link Car Sharing

The link app is free to use and allows drivers and riders to post their trips. It also takes 10% of every application fee to plant a native New Zealand tree.

Pain Point:

  • Poor User Interface: The current user interface of the website may not be very intuitive or user-friendly, leading to confusion and frustration for users who are trying to book a car or manage their account.
  • Limited Trip Availability: Some users may find that there are not enough trips available for their daily commute to work or school. This may limit the usefulness of the service for these users.

USER FLOW & USER STORIES

After conducting user and market research, creating user stories can be a helpful tool to showcase the key features of the app from a user's perspective. These user stories can then be transformed into user flows using Figma, which provide a visual representation of how users would interact with the app to accomplish their goals.

This approach can help ensure that the app's design is aligned with user needs and expectations, ultimately leading to a more satisfying user experience.

User Story 1

"As a driver, the user wants to be able to post a ride to their workplace, so that they can connect with potential carpooling partners and reduce their commuting costs. By offering a ride, the user hopes to find one or more passengers who are traveling in the same direction and willing to share the cost of the trip. This will help the driver save money on gas and reduce their carbon footprint, while also providing a more sociable and enjoyable commuting experience."

User Story 2

"As a commuter without a car, the user wants to be able to search for available rides to their workplace, so that they can save time and money on their daily commute. By finding a ride through the app, the user hopes to be able to travel to work in a safe and comfortable manner, without having to worry about the hassle and expense of public transportation. The user may also be looking for a more social and engaging commuting experience, by connecting with drivers and fellow passengers who share similar interests and lifestyles."

VISUAL DESIGN

Logo design:

The logo design for the carpooling app incorporates a location icon and one-way text, symbolising the shared use of a common route by multiple users.

The location icon represents the app's focus on facilitating transportation between locations, while the one-way text emphasises the app's mission of promoting sustainable, one-way journeys through carpooling.

The combination of these elements creates a visually striking and memorable logo that effectively communicates the core values and purpose of the carpooling app.

Design system:

Following the user and market research, a design system was developed for the one-way carpool application and website.

The design system encompasses a collection of design elements, principles, and guidelines used consistently throughout the project to ensure a cohesive and user-friendly experience. The design system includes typography, icons, color schemes, buttons, and patterns that have been carefully selected and crafted to enhance the user experience and create a visually appealing interface.

The design system ensures consistency across all pages and devices, making it easy for users to navigate and engage with the one-way carpool application and website. A style guide is included to showcase the various design elements and guidelines used in the design system.

LOW-FIDELITY PROTOTYPES

Using the user flows as a guide, I created low-fidelity prototypes on Figma to illustrate how to post a ride on the one-way carpool app. The app's bottom menu features four sections: FIND RIDES, MY RIDES, CHATS, and SETTINGS.

In the FIND RIDES section, users can quickly search for available rides or post a ride for their specific time. Whether a user is a driver, a rider, or flexible depending on the situation, the app caters to their needs.

The MY RIDES section provides users with a comprehensive view of their upcoming and past rides. It's easy to access all the information they need about their trip, including the cost, which is automatically calculated based on the pick-up and drop-off locations.

Additionally, the app's chat function enables users to easily communicate with other users and make changes to their trip as needed. This feature adds flexibility and convenience to the carpooling experience.

USABILITY TESTING

To ensure that the ride-sharing app I designed was user-friendly and intuitive, I conducted usability testing with a group of representative users. I used a low-fidelity prototype to test the basic functionality of the app and gather feedback on its usability.

During the usability testing, I first asked the users about their experience using ride-sharing apps and briefly introduced them to the oneway carpool app. I then gave them specific tasks to perform, such as posting a ride as a driver and finding a driver to book a ride with, and asked them to find their pending trips and use the app's chat function. I observed how users interacted with the app and listened to their feedback to determine whether the lo-fi prototype was effective in communicating the app's basic features and functionality.

Based on the feedback from the usability testing, the users were able to complete the tasks and understand the user flow. This indicates that the lo-fi prototype was successful in communicating the app's basic features and functionality.

HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPES

After conducting user testing on the low-fidelity prototype, I used the feedback gathered to create high-fidelity prototypes. These prototypes provided a more detailed and polished version of the ride-sharing app's user interface, and were developed using design software Figma.

Given that the driver and rider have different needs and user flows, I created two main user flows: one for drivers on how to post a ride, and another for riders on how to find a ride. I made sure that each flow was tailored to the specific needs of each user type, providing a seamless and intuitive experience.

To showcase the high-fidelity prototypes, an interactive prototype is available for viewing below. This prototype allows users to interact with the app as if it were a fully functioning product, giving them a better sense of how the app would look and feel in use.

FINAL DESIGN

Quick to find the rides

  • The oneway carpool app offers a seamless and efficient ride-sharing experience.
  • Users can quickly browse available rides or post their own ride with specific requirements.
  • The app caters to the needs of both drivers and riders, as well as those who are flexible with their travel arrangements.
  • With its user-friendly interface and efficient algorithms, the app helps users save time, money, and energy while promoting sustainable transportation options.

Easy to check your rides

  • The oneway carpool app makes checking upcoming and past rides simple and convenient.
  • The My Ride section of the app provides all the necessary information about the trip, including pickup/drop-off locations and ride cost.
  • Users can check rider/driver references and leave reviews to ensure a safe and enjoyable ride experience.
  • The app is designed to make ride-sharing stress-free and efficient, with a user-friendly interface and helpful features for all users.

Efficient to chat with your carpooler

  • The oneway carpool app offers a convenient chat function for efficient communication between drivers and riders.
  • Users receive notifications from the official account, keeping them informed about their trip details.
  • The chat function allows users to ask questions and clarify any details about the trip.
  • Efficient communication through the app ensures a smooth and stress-free ride experience for all users.

TAKEAWAYS & NEXT STEPS

Through this project, I learned that the design process requires iteration and may involve going back and forth several times before finding the right solution. And User research and testing are crucial for improving the design's usability and accessibility.

If I had more time to improve this project, some next steps would be:

  • Differentiate the interface more clearly for the rider and driver to avoid confusion.
  • Explore how the interface could look when the driver is in the process of picking up riders.
  • Research how users would prefer to make payments to share costs.
